Overland Trade · c. 1st century BCE – 14th century CE

The Silk Road

A network of oasis cities, multilingual documents, migrations, faiths, and material objects — not a single highway, but a living web of connections across Eurasia.

This project traces the Silk Road through its primary sources: contracts, court records, wooden tablets, coins, and cave paintings left behind in the sands of Central Asia.

Iranian Worlds

The Silk Road was not a Chinese or a Western story. Its primary agents of connection were Iranian peoples — whose languages, faiths, and commercial networks held Eurasia together for centuries.

Objects

The Silk Road is visible through what it left behind — not only luxury goods, but the documents, coins, and everyday objects of oasis life.
